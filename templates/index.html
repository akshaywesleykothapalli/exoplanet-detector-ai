About Exoplanet Detection

What are Exoplanets?

Exoplanets are planets that orbit stars outside our solar system. Scientists study them to understand planetary systems and the possibility of life beyond Earth.

How Are Exoplanets Detected?

This project uses the transit method, where a planet passing in front of a star causes a small dip in brightness that can be measured and analyzed.

What Does This AI Do?

The AI analyzes astronomical parameters and predicts whether an observed signal is likely caused by a real exoplanet or noise.

Machine Learning Model

A Random Forest classifier is trained on labeled exoplanet data to make accurate predictions with high reliability.

Input Parameters Used

Orbital period, planet radius, transit depth, transit duration, and orbital eccentricity are used to classify candidates.

Model Accuracy

The trained model achieves approximately 94% accuracy on test data, making it suitable for exploratory scientific analysis.

Why This Project Matters

This project demonstrates how AI can assist astronomers by accelerating planet discovery and reducing false positives in space research.
